Egg Omelette

An omelette is a quick and delicious egg-based dish made by beating eggs and cooking them in a pan, often with fillings like cheese, vegetables, or herbs. It's perfect for breakfast, lunch, or a light dinner.

Preparation

  • Total :approximately 10minutes
  • Preparation : 5 minutes
  • Cooking : 5 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2–3 eggs
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Pepper (to taste)
  • 1 tbsp milk or water (optional, for fluffiness)
  • 1 tsp oil or butter
  • Optional fillings: chopped onions, tomatoes, cheese, spinach, mushrooms, herbs, etc.

Instructions

  1. Crack the eggs into a bowl. Add salt, pepper, and milk/water (if using). Beat well with a fork.
  2. Heat a pan on medium heat. Add oil or butter and let it melt.
  3. Pour the egg mixture into the pan. Swirl to spread evenly.
  4. Let it cook for 1–2 minutes until the edges start to set. You can gently lift the edges and tilt the pan to let uncooked egg flow underneath.
  5. Add fillings (if any) on one half of the omelette.
  6. Fold it in half once the bottom is set and the top is just slightly runny.
  7. Slide it out onto a plate. Serve hot!

Nutritions

  • Omelettes are rich in high-quality protein, essential for muscle repair and growth.
  • Using whole eggs provides good fats, especially omega-3s if pasture-raised eggs are used.
  • Eggs contain vitamins A, D, B12, folate, and minerals like selenium and iron.
  • Great for low-carb or keto diets when made without bread or starchy fillings.
  • You can boost fiber, antioxidants, and vitamins by adding vegetables, herbs, or even lean meats.
